GYM EQUIPMENT GUIDE

CARDIO EQUIPMENT

Treadmill: A motorized machine for walking, jogging, or running indoors. Adjust speed from 0.5-15 mph and incline up to 15%. Great for cardiovascular health, weight loss, and endurance training. Always start slow and gradually increase intensity.

Elliptical Machine: Low-impact cardio equipment that works both upper and lower body. Provides excellent cardiovascular workout while being gentle on joints. Ideal for people with knee or hip problems. Burn 300-400 calories in 30 minutes.

Stationary Bike: Indoor cycling equipment available in upright and recumbent styles. Upright bikes mimic outdoor cycling, while recumbent bikes provide back support. Excellent for leg strength and cardiovascular fitness.

Rowing Machine: Full-body cardio equipment that works 85% of your muscles. Provides excellent cardiovascular and strength training. Proper form is crucial - drive with legs, pull with back, finish with arms.

StairMaster: Simulates stair climbing for intense lower body and cardiovascular workout. Burns high calories and builds leg strength. Start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase duration.

STRENGTH TRAINING EQUIPMENT

Barbell: Long steel bar used with weight plates for compound exercises. Standard Olympic barbell weighs 45 pounds. Essential for exercises like squats, deadlifts, bench press, and rows.

Dumbbells: Versatile free weights available in various weights. Allow for unilateral training and full range of motion. Start with lighter weights to master form before progressing.

Weight Plates: Iron or rubber-coated discs that attach to barbells and machines. Standard plates: 2.5, 5, 10, 25, 35, and 45 pounds. Always load plates evenly on both sides.

Cable Machine: Pulley system with adjustable heights and various attachments. Provides constant tension throughout movement. Excellent for isolation exercises and functional training.

Smith Machine: Barbell fixed on vertical tracks with safety catches. Provides stability for beginners but limits natural movement patterns. Good for learning proper squat and bench press form.

Power Rack/Squat Rack: Steel frame with adjustable safety bars and pull-up bars. Essential for safe heavy lifting. Always set safety bars at appropriate height for your exercise.

Bench Press: Flat, incline, or decline bench with barbell supports. Fundamental equipment for chest development. Always use a spotter when lifting heavy weights.

Leg Press Machine: Seated machine for lower body strength training. Allows heavy loading with reduced spinal stress. Keep feet shoulder-width apart and knees aligned with toes.

Lat Pulldown Machine: Cable machine for back development. Targets latissimus dorsi muscles. Pull bar to upper chest, not behind neck, to avoid injury.

EXERCISE GUIDE

CHEST EXERCISES

Bench Press: Lie on bench, grip barbell slightly wider than shoulders. Lower bar to chest, press up explosively. Keep shoulder blades retracted and feet planted. Start with bodyweight equivalent.

Push-ups: Start in plank position, lower chest to floor, push back up. Modify on knees if needed. Progress to decline push-ups for increased difficulty.

Dumbbell Flyes: Lie on bench with dumbbells above chest. Lower weights in arc motion, feeling stretch in chest. Bring dumbbells together above chest.

Incline Bench Press: Performed on inclined bench (30-45 degrees) to target upper chest. Use same technique as flat bench press but with adjusted angle.

BACK EXERCISES

Pull-ups/Chin-ups: Hang from bar, pull body up until chin clears bar. Pull-ups use overhand grip, chin-ups use underhand grip. Assist with resistance bands if needed.

Bent-over Rows: Hinge at hips, keep back straight. Pull barbell or dumbbells to lower chest/upper abdomen. Squeeze shoulder blades together at top.

Lat Pulldowns: Sit at machine, grip bar wider than shoulders. Pull bar to upper chest while leaning slightly back. Control the weight on the way up.

Deadlifts: Stand with feet hip-width apart, barbell over midfoot. Hinge at hips, keep back straight, lift bar by driving through heels. Most important exercise for posterior chain.

LEG EXERCISES

Squats: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out. Lower by sitting back and down, keep chest up. Descend until thighs parallel to floor, then drive up through heels.

Lunges: Step forward into long stride, lower back knee toward ground. Keep front knee over ankle. Push off front foot to return to start. Alternate legs.

Leg Press: Sit in machine with feet on platform shoulder-width apart. Lower weight by bending knees to 90 degrees, then press through heels to extend legs.

Calf Raises: Stand on balls of feet on platform or step. Raise up on toes as high as possible, hold briefly, then lower slowly. Can be done with bodyweight or added weight.

SHOULDER EXERCISES

Overhead Press: Stand with feet hip-width apart, press barbell or dumbbells overhead. Keep core tight and avoid arching back excessively.

Lateral Raises: Hold dumbbells at sides, raise arms out to sides until parallel to floor. Control the movement and avoid using momentum.

Rear Delt Flyes: Bend forward at hips, raise dumbbells out to sides while squeezing shoulder blades. Targets rear deltoids and improves posture.

ARM EXERCISES

Bicep Curls: Hold dumbbells or barbell with underhand grip. Curl weight up by flexing biceps, lower slowly. Keep elbows at sides throughout movement.

Tricep Dips: Support body on bench or parallel bars. Lower body by bending elbows, then press back up. Keep body close to bench/bars.

Close-Grip Bench Press: Perform bench press with hands closer together (about shoulder-width). Targets triceps more than regular bench press.

CORE EXERCISES

Planks: Hold body in straight line from head to heels, supporting on forearms and toes. Keep core tight and breathe normally. Start with 30 seconds, progress to 2 minutes.

Crunches: Lie on back, knees bent, hands behind head. Curl upper body toward knees using abdominal muscles. Avoid pulling on neck.

Russian Twists: Sit with knees bent, lean back slightly. Rotate torso side to side, touching ground beside hips. Add weight for increased difficulty.

Mountain Climbers: Start in plank position, alternate bringing knees to chest rapidly. Keep core tight and maintain plank position throughout.

NUTRITION AND FITNESS

MACRONUTRIENTS

Protein: Essential for muscle building and repair. Aim for 0.8-1.2g per pound of bodyweight daily. Best sources: lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, protein powder.

Carbohydrates: Primary energy source for workouts. Focus on complex carbs: oats, rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes. Time carb intake around workouts for optimal performance.

Fats: Important for hormone production and vitamin absorption. Include healthy fats: nuts, olive oil, avocados, fatty fish. Aim for 20-30% of total calories.

HYDRATION

Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily, more during intense training. Proper hydration improves performance, recovery, and prevents fatigue. Drink water before, during, and after workouts.

PRE-WORKOUT NUTRITION

Eat 1-2 hours before training. Include carbohydrates for energy and some protein. Good options: banana with peanut butter, oatmeal with berries, Greek yogurt with granola.

POST-WORKOUT NUTRITION

Consume protein and carbohydrates within 30-60 minutes after training to optimize recovery. Aim for 20-30g protein and 30-50g carbohydrates. Chocolate milk, protein shake with banana, or chicken with rice are excellent options.

SUPPLEMENTS

Whey Protein: Convenient way to meet daily protein requirements. Take 20-30g post-workout or between meals.

Creatine: Improves strength and power output. Take 3-5g daily, timing doesn't matter. Most researched and effective supplement for strength training.

Multivitamin: Covers potential nutrient gaps in diet. Choose reputable brand with third-party testing.

Fish Oil: Supports joint health and reduces inflammation. Take 1-2g EPA/DHA daily.

SAFETY AND FORM

WARM-UP

Always start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio followed by dynamic stretching. Gradually increase intensity to prepare muscles and joints for exercise.

PROPER FORM

Focus on quality over quantity. Poor form leads to injury and reduces exercise effectiveness. Start with lighter weights to master movement patterns.

BREATHING

Exhale during the exertion phase (lifting), inhale during the lowering phase. Never hold your breath during heavy lifting as it can cause dangerous blood pressure spikes.

SPOTTING

Use a spotter for heavy bench press, squats, and overhead exercises. Spotter should be experienced and ready to assist if needed.

REST BETWEEN SETS

Allow 30-60 seconds rest for endurance/fat loss, 60-90 seconds for hypertrophy, 2-3 minutes for strength training.

PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD

Gradually increase weight, reps, or sets over time to continue making progress. Track your workouts to ensure consistent progression.

RECOVERY

Allow 48-72 hours between training the same muscle groups. Muscles grow during rest, not during workouts. Prioritize sleep (7-9 hours nightly) for optimal recovery.

GYM ETIQUETTE

EQUIPMENT SHARING

Allow others to work in between your sets if they ask politely. Offer to let others work in if you're resting long periods.

CLEANING EQUIPMENT

Wipe down equipment after use with provided sanitizing wipes. This shows respect for other gym members and maintains hygiene.

RERACKING WEIGHTS

Always return weights to their proper location after use. Strip all weight plates from barbells and machines.

NOISE LEVELS

Avoid dropping weights unnecessarily or making excessive noise. Grunt quietly if needed, but avoid disturbing others.

PERSONAL SPACE

Respect others' workout space. Don't stand too close to someone exercising or block their view of mirrors.

PHONE USAGE

Keep phone calls brief and away from workout areas. Avoid occupying equipment while on your phone.

MEMBERSHIP INFORMATION

GYM HOURS

Most gyms operate from 5 AM to 11 PM on weekdays, with reduced hours on weekends. 24-hour gyms provide access anytime with key card entry.

MEMBERSHIP TYPES

Day Pass: $10-20 for single day access
Monthly Membership: $20-50 per month depending on amenities
Annual Membership: Often includes 1-2 months free compared to monthly rates
Family Plans: Discounts for multiple family members
Student Discounts: 10-20% off regular rates with valid student ID

GUEST POLICIES

Most gyms allow 1-2 guest visits per month with member accompaniment. Some charge $5-10 guest fees. Guests must sign waivers and follow all gym rules.

CANCELLATION POLICIES

Read cancellation terms carefully. Many require 30-day written notice. Some have early termination fees. Month-to-month memberships offer more flexibility.

AMENITIES

Locker Rooms: Secure lockers, showers, changing areas. Bring your own lock and toiletries.
Group Classes: Yoga, spinning, Zumba, CrossFit. Often included in membership or small additional fee.
Personal Training: One-on-one instruction. Rates vary from $30-100+ per session.
Childcare: Some gyms offer supervised childcare during workout hours.
Pool/Sauna: Additional amenities at premium facilities.

INJURY PREVENTION

COMMON GYM INJURIES

Lower Back Strain: Often from poor deadlift or squat form. Keep neutral spine and engage core.
Shoulder Impingement: From overhead exercises with poor form. Warm up shoulders thoroughly.
Knee Injuries: From improper squatting or jumping. Keep knees aligned with toes.
Wrist Strain: From poor grip or wrist position during pressing movements.

WARNING SIGNS

Stop exercising immediately if you experience:
- Sharp, shooting pain
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Excessive fatigue beyond normal workout tiredness
- Joint swelling or severe soreness

INJURY PREVENTION TIPS

Warm up properly before each workout
Use full range of motion in exercises
Progress gradually - don't rush to heavy weights
Listen to your body and take rest days when needed
Stay hydrated throughout your workout
Cool down and stretch after training

WHEN TO SEEK HELP

Consult a fitness professional if you're unsure about proper form
See a doctor for persistent pain or injuries
Get a fitness assessment if you're new to exercise
Consider physical therapy for recurring injuries

GOAL-SPECIFIC TRAINING

WEIGHT LOSS

Combine strength training with cardio for best results. Aim for 3-4 strength sessions and 3-5 cardio sessions per week. Create caloric deficit through diet and exercise.

MUSCLE BUILDING

Focus on progressive overload with compound exercises. Train each muscle group 2-3 times per week. Eat in caloric surplus with adequate protein intake.

STRENGTH GAINS

Emphasize heavy compound movements (squats, deadlifts, bench press). Use lower rep ranges (1-5 reps) with longer rest periods (3-5 minutes).

ENDURANCE

Incorporate longer cardio sessions and higher rep strength training. Gradually increase workout duration and intensity over time.

SPORTS PERFORMANCE

Include sport-specific movements and plyometric exercises. Focus on power, agility, and functional strength training.
